It's June and the evenings are finally long enough that a ride after work doesn't end in the dark. Spring stuck the landing. I'm a little sunburned and very happy about it.

## I maintain a scroll wheel now

So this happened. I'm now the [nixpkgs](https://github.com/NixOS/nixpkgs) maintainer for **UnnaturalScrollWheels**- the lil macOS utility that inverts scroll direction for a mouse independently of your trackpad, which is the single most important quality-of-life fix for anyone who has ever plugged a normal mouse into a Mac and felt their soul leave their body.

It's a small package. It is not a small feeling. There's something genuinely lovely about the Nix model here- somebody has to actually own the derivation, keep the hash current, bump the version when upstream ships- and now, for this one tiny thing that I use every single day, that somebody is me. I went looking for the package to fix something, found it needed a hand, and... well. Now I'm holding the hand. That's kind of the whole ethos, isn't it. You use the thing, you notice the thing needs care, you become the person who cares for it.

Maintaining open-source infrastructure for a mouse-scroll utility is a deeply unglamorous honor and I will be insufferable about it for at least a week.

## Spring legs (and then some)

The bike is back and so am I. I've been putting in **100 miles a week** and my legs have stopped filing formal complaints about it- that lovely moment in the season where the base miles finally compound and a ride that would've wrecked me in March just feels like Tuesday.

A hundred a week is the number where it stops being "I should ride more" and starts being a thing my body expects. Miss a day and it sulks. I love that. I love being a person my own legs nag.

## ...So about 2030

Here's the thing I haven't said out loud much yet, so I'm saying it here where it'll be embarrassing to back out of.

I want to do **Chase the Sun**- the across-Ireland one- in **2030**.

For the uninitiated: you start at one coast at sunrise and you ride to the other coast and you try to beat the sun to the far sea. The whole island, in a day, against a clock you cannot negotiate with. It's exactly the kind of beautiful, slightly unhinged endurance objective that I cannot stop thinking about once it's lodged in my brain.

2030 is far enough away that it sounds reasonable and close enough that the training is, like, now. The 100-mile weeks aren't really about this week. They're the first deposits on a four-years-out withdrawal. Coast to coast, dawn to dusk, beat the sun. We'll see.

That's the week. Scroll wheels fixed, legs found, sun officially being chased.

-Jess
